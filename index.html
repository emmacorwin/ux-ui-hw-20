OVERVIEW

This design sprint partnered with the Colorado Animal Rescue. My team, with fellow designers, Alika Recek and Madi Mathews, felt compelled to focus our non-profit redesign on the C.A.R. because the current web design is limiting the amount of community engagement in the shelter, and also the potential for the pets of being adopted. Issues like a visibile search bar, detailed and organized pet cards, easy to access resources, navigation buttons where you would expect them to be, the list goes on. Given the abundance of complications we found in their current site design, it was clear that their web presence was not matching their professionalism as a shelter and their passion for the pets.

THE PROBLEM

A need to get people more involved in volunteering and successfully integrate an animal into our user’s family.

We observed that our product isn’t meeting the expectation of users for full transparency and easily navigable information.

OUR SOLUTION

By improving the experience on our website for perspective pet parents and volunteers, we will build the trust and professionalism our organization needs to connect our pets to their people.

USER RESEARCH

Five user interviews were conducted to collect qualitative data on how people experience adopting and fostering pets. A survey was also used to gather quantitative data. This data was analyzed to understand how the Colorado Animal Resue’s current design is falling short.

Cassie Hahn

Cassie is a teacher located in Glenwood Springs, Colorado and she is searching to adopt a dog. She likes to volunteer and thinks that may be an avenue to finding her future pet. In her free time she enjoys being outside taking walks and going on hikes. She would love to find a dog that is friendly around people, mid-size, and is energetic to keep up with her hobbies.

NEEDS
no kill shelter
up to date on vaccinations
Cannot be "unsafe, unclean, unfriendly, unhealthy."
Needs professionalism, transparency and passionate people at the shelter

WANTS
wants a companion to join on her excursions
has always wanted to adopt
likes to have something to stand behind.

FINDINGS

We found the integrity, honesty and passion of the shelter are of the utmost importance for our user. They want to trust the information and trust that the shelter prioritizes the health and well-being of each pet.

INFORMATION STRUCTURE

Of the various avenues of involvement in the shelter, our users are most interested in adopting. Highlighting the pets and making their cards as beautiful and easy to access became our top priority.The animal search and adoption flow was our focus.

USER FLOW

For this flow, the user has access to simplified pet cards when scrolling on the main screen, where they are provided info on certain highlighted pets. They are also able to navigate to the adopt page that displays at the top of the main navigation.
The adoption page provides different sections for the various types of pets. While the adoption page offers further sorting, both the home screen and the adoption page allow for beautiful pet cards, important info on the pets, and navigation to the adoption application form. After completing a simple two page application, the users submit their forms and await contact from the Colorado Animal Rescue.

STYLE GUIDE

This is a sampling of our style guide. It is a playful and friendly design paired with clean and sophisticated photos with overlays.
